# Marketing Against the Grain

## Hosts
- **Kipp Bodnar** — CMO, HubSpot
- **Kieran Flanagan** — SVP of Marketing, HubSpot (also former CMO of Zapier)

## Podcast Overview
A HubSpot Original Podcast. Kipp and Kieran take listeners behind the scenes of how top marketers are actually thinking about growth — not the recycled Twitter-thread advice, but genuinely contrarian, first-principles thinking. Episodes release semi-weekly.

**Where to listen:** Spotify, Apple Podcasts, YouTube

## Episodes Referenced

### Episode 418: The Rise of Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)
- Key topic: AI search tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) are already recommending competitors to your customers — and most companies don't know it
- Kipp introduces AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) as the evolution of SEO — optimizing your brand to appear in AI-generated answers, not just Google results
- HubSpot's own AEO playbook made them the #1 most visible CRM in AI search
- **LinkedIn content angle:** SaaS companies that educate their audience about AEO on LinkedIn now will be ahead of the market in 12–18 months

### Episode 419: 250 Pieces of Content Per Week, Zero Employees
- Guest: Sabrina Ramonov of Blotato — built a 2 million-person audience as a solo creator using AI workflows
- Key ideas: Building a repeatable brand voice with AI, automating content from raw images, managing a full social calendar through smart connectors and tools
- **LinkedIn content angle:** The era of "I don't have a team" is over as an excuse. AI can power a content engine for one person or a small agency like Yokomet.

### Episode: B2B Creator Economy Rising
- Kipp and Kieran discuss why the cost of marketing has gone up 108% in 10 years
- B2B is increasingly shifting toward creator-style content — founders, employees, and executives building personal audiences rather than relying on brand pages
- Key idea: The B2B creator playbook (consistent POV, human voice, niche audience) is outperforming traditional demand gen

### Episode: How to Guarantee AI Won't Replace You
- Two out of three jobs will be automatable through AI
- The moat is not knowing AI tools — it's having a point of view, creative judgment, and audience trust
- Marketers who build personal brands and real communities are harder to replace

## Key Ideas (Relevant to LinkedIn Organic Content for B2B SaaS)

- **AEO is the next frontier for B2B SaaS visibility:** As AI tools replace Google as the discovery layer, B2B brands need to be cited and mentioned in AI training data and web sources. LinkedIn content that is cited, shared, and referenced feeds into that ecosystem.
- **Distribution is a product:** Kipp and Kieran consistently frame content not as "marketing" but as a distribution engine. The SaaS companies winning on LinkedIn treat their content calendar like a product roadmap — built for specific audiences, tested, and iterated.
- **Brand before product messaging:** One of the most repeated ideas on MATG — people follow people and ideas before they follow products. LinkedIn organic works the same way.
- **Solo creator efficiency is a competitive advantage:** Small B2B teams that adopt AI-powered content workflows can compete with larger marketing teams. The advantage is speed and authenticity, not budget.

## Why This Podcast Matters for LinkedIn Organic Strategy

Marketing Against the Grain is arguably the most consistently forward-looking B2B marketing podcast. What makes it relevant for LinkedIn content strategy:

1. **They model what they teach** — Kipp and Kieran actively post on LinkedIn. Their content style (hot takes, short frameworks, genuine opinions) is a direct case study.
2. **Contrarian framing drives engagement** — "Marketing Against the Grain" as a positioning is itself a LinkedIn content lesson: lead with the counterintuitive angle.
3. **AI content workflows are now table-stakes** — Episodes on AI-powered content creation show that B2B SaaS brands need a documented, repeatable content system — not just individual posts.
4. **AEO + LinkedIn = future of B2B discoverability** — As AI search grows, LinkedIn posts that are insightful and shareable feed into a brand's overall discoverability. It's not just a social play, it's an SEO/AEO play.
