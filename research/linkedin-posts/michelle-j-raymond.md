## Post 2 — 3 days ago (at time of research)

Quick tip for Company Page Admins — if you're 
gonna tag a colleague in your brand post check 
these things first:

1. Is their profile complete? At least banner, 
   About section, current photo we can see.
2. Will they ever log on and engage with the 
   post? If they won't, don't tag them. It's 
   a bad look and it will crush what little 
   engagement you could have gotten.
3. If it's someone from your Executive or 
   Leadership team that you consistently tag, 
   then invest in getting their profile sorted 
   properly.

Power of Two in action — employee and company 
brands work together. Each reflects on the 
other and either works to get you closer to 
your goals or takes a step back.

If it's not obvious — dodgy profiles are 
taking you a step back.

NB this is not me saying the company owns 
the employees personal profile and has 
control over what they should post. But 
in my experience most people are grateful 
for the help — especially at the top.
