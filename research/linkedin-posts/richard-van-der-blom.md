## Post 3 — 2 days ago (at time of research)

*Post included a detailed infographic titled 
"Lifespan of a LinkedIn Post" from his 
Algorithm Insights Report 2026 — showing 
4 post lifespan patterns with a graph 
tracking daily reach distribution from 
H1 to D14.*

Not all LinkedIn posts die the same way.
Some explode and vanish. Others build slowly.
A few even come back from the dead.

After analysing 1.3M posts, we found 4 
distinct lifespan patterns:

🚀 The Rocket (45% of posts)
Strong out of the gate. 40% of reach lands 
within 4 hours, 70% within day one. Burns 
fast — done by day 5.

How to nurture it:
→ Reply to every comment in the first 4 hours
→ Ask follow-up questions to keep threads alive
→ Don't wait — the window closes fast

📈 The Builder (30% of posts)
Modest start, steady climb. Engagement builds 
through day 1 and sustains to day 8. Save 
rates are disproportionately high.

How to nurture it:
→ Re-enter the thread on days 2-3 with 
  a fresh insight
→ Reply to comments with depth, not just thanks
→ Share the post to your newsletter or 
  other channels

🌱 The Grower (20% of posts)
Slow to start, long to run. Only 30% reach 
on day one, then pulses across two weeks 
driven by saves and interest cluster 
resurfacing.

How to nurture it:
→ Don't abandon after 48 hours — it's 
  just warming up
→ Comment something valuable on day 3-4 
  to trigger redistribution
→ Let it breathe — don't post over it 
  too quickly

🔥 The Afterburner (5% of posts)
Underperforms days 1-2, then ignites. A 
share, comment, or save triggers a spike 
on day 3-4 and sustained distribution 
for two weeks.

How to nurture it:
→ Double down when it ignites — reply 
  aggressively
→ Add a comment with additional context 
  or data
→ Share it yourself to accelerate the 
  second wave

This is just 1 of 100+ visuals and tactics 
in the Algorithm Insights Report 2026.

## My Observations

Richard's third post was the most valuable 
find in this entire research. The fact that 
he analysed 1.3 million posts to identify 
4 distinct lifespan patterns shows the 
depth of his research. This is not LinkedIn 
advice, this is LinkedIn science.

The most important insight for 100Hires: 
45% of posts are Rockets that burn out in 
5 days, but 20% are Growers that pulse 
for two weeks. This means 100Hires should 
not judge a post's performance in the 
first 48 hours, some content takes time 
to find its audience.

His first post also confirmed something 
critical: posting frequently without clear 
added value is now actively hurting accounts 
on LinkedIn. For 100Hires this means quality 
and niche relevance must come before volume 
in any LinkedIn content strategy.
