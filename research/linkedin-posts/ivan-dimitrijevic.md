## Post 3 — Recent post (at time of research)

*Post included a visual quote image: 
"Visibility fills the room. 
Credibility moves the deal."*

Getting seen is easier than getting believed.

Most B2B teams still act like those are 
the same thing. They are not.

A company can be highly visible and still 
hard to choose. You can have:
- Reach
- Traffic
- Followers
- Engagement
- Share of voice

And still lose.

Because visibility is not the same thing 
as buyability.

Buyability comes from signal quality:
- Clear positioning
- Specific proof
- Sharp messaging
- Coherent team presence
- Operator credibility
- Confidence that survives scrutiny

A lot of brands are easy to spot and 
hard to explain. Seen often. Chosen rarely.

The market sees them. But cannot form 
a strong reason to choose them. And in 
B2B, that is fatal.

Because buying groups do not just ask:
"Who have we heard of?"

They ask:
"Who feels credible, clear, safe, and 
worth defending internally?"

Visibility fills the room.
Credibility moves the deal.
Attention gets you considered.
Believability gets you chosen.

## My Observations

After reading these 3 posts, what stands out 
about Ivan is that he does not talk about 
LinkedIn tactics, he talks about the psychology 
of B2B buying. His "Invisible Shortlist System" 
concept was the most striking insight: by the 
time a buyer reaches out, they have already 
decided who they trust. This means LinkedIn 
content needs to work long before a lead 
appears in any CRM.

For 100Hires specifically, this means the 
company needs to be building trust with HR 
managers and startup founders continuously ,
not just when they are actively selling. 
The content strategy needs to work at the 
Exposure and Familiarity stages, not just 
at the bottom of the funnel.
