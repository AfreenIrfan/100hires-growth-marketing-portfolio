## Research Methodology

I spent April 22, 2026 researching and verifying 
experts for this topic. My process:

- Searched LinkedIn manually using specific 
  keywords like "LinkedIn content strategy B2B SaaS" 
  and "LinkedIn B2B pipeline" to find relevant voices
- Rejected several well-known names like Lara Acosta, 
  Justin Welsh and Jasmin Alic after reviewing their 
  content and finding it too focused on personal 
  branding rather than B2B SaaS specifically
- Verified each LinkedIn profile individually — 
  checking follower count, bio, recent posts and 
  engagement before confirming inclusion
- Searched YouTube for B2B SaaS LinkedIn content 
  creators and reviewed channel pages directly
- Found podcast experts through TopRank B2B 
  Marketing's curated list and cross-referenced 
  their relevance to our topic
- Used Claude AI as a research assistant — similar 
  to using Google — to get suggestions, push back 
  on weak choices, and structure findings. All 
  final decisions and verifications were done by me.

The biggest challenge was avoiding generic names. 
Several well-known creators were rejected because 
their content did not specifically address LinkedIn 
organic strategy for B2B SaaS. The final 10 were 
chosen because they either work directly with B2B 
SaaS companies, publish data-backed research, or 
have built measurable revenue through LinkedIn 
content specifically.

---

## Expert Selection Criteria

1. They actively post or create content themselves
   — not just write about it theoretically
2. They work specifically with B2B or SaaS companies
3. They share data, results and frameworks — 
   not just opinions or motivation
4. Minimum 20K followers for LinkedIn experts
   to ensure credibility signals

Generic names and first Google results were 
deliberately avoided. Every expert was manually 
verified by reviewing their profile, recent content, 
engagement and relevance to B2B SaaS LinkedIn 
strategy specifically.
