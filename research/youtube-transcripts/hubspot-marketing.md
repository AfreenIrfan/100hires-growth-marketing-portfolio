## Core Insights Extracted

### Strategy 1 — Look Easy to Connect With

Your LinkedIn profile is not a resume.
It is a landing page.

Three profile fixes:
- Headline should say HOW you help, not just
  your job title. Bad: "Business Development
  at Acme." Good: "Helping RevOps leaders turn
  customers into case studies."
- Profile photo should look open, friendly,
  approachable — not corporate or salesy
- Banner should act like a billboard — tell
  people what they will get if they follow you
- About section should highlight wins and
  results, written for customers not peers

### Strategy 2 — Stop Playing the InMail Lottery

Most people send mass connection requests with
zero context. This is wrong. Instead:

- Send connection requests with intention
- Always add a personalised note explaining
  WHY you want to connect
- Never use generic notes like "LinkedIn
  suggested I connect with you"
- Target people who have already engaged with
  your content — they have context, they are
  warm leads
- Try to have mutual connections with someone
  at their company before connecting with your
  target person

### Strategy 3 — The One-Sentence DM Framework

Nobody reads essay-long DMs. Treat LinkedIn
DMs like text messages, not emails.

The framework:
- Make ONE observation about the person
- Ask ONE open-ended question
- Keep it to 1-2 lines maximum

Examples from the video:
- "I noticed you're hiring for this role. 
  Want to know how I might help you find 
  the right person?"
- "Looks like you're creating a lot of 
  content. Have you tested video? I think 
  we could 10x your impressions."

Avoid yes/no questions. Ask open-ended
questions that get people talking.

### Strategy 4 — Pattern Interrupts in Follow-Ups

Instead of following up with the same text
message five times, change the medium:

- Send a 30-second voice note instead
- Send a casual selfie video
- Go live on LinkedIn and host a Q&A

These cannot be automated at scale. In a world
of AI-generated follow-ups, human voice and
video stands out instantly and gets the highest
response rates.

### Strategy 5 — Build Trust With Content

The Four E Framework for LinkedIn content:
- Educate — teach people things related to 
  what you sell
- Engage — create two-way conversations
- Entertain — memes, relatable content
- Empower — celebrate client successes,
  share results and screenshots

Identify 3-5 content pillars — themes you
want associated with your brand. Post video,
written, and image content around these
pillars every week consistently.

Key principle: Create stories once and
distribute them forever.

### Strategy 6 — Lean Into Video

The media hierarchy for building trust:
- Text builds authority
- Audio builds connection
- Video builds trust fastest

When people watch you speak on camera
regularly, by the time they meet you at a
conference they feel like they already know
you. Video creates familiarity at scale.

Practical video formats:
- 30-60 second selfie videos sharing one
  lesson or story
- Behind the scenes of your team
- Product demos recorded on screen
- Event recaps
- Short video clips sent directly in DMs

### Strategy 7 — Capture Attention Fast

The hook is everything. If the first line
does not stop someone from scrolling,
nothing else matters.

A good hook:
- Stops the scroll with curiosity or a
  bold claim
- Uses specific numbers
- Signals relevance to the reader's role
- Calls out the exact job title of the
  person you want to reach

The goal of every post is to get people
to click "see more." Everything else
comes after that.
