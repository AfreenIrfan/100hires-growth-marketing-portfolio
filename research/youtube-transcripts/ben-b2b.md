## Core Insights Extracted

### The B2B Marketing Problem in 2026

71% of B2B marketers think their messaging is
unique. 68% of buyers say all brands sound the
same. Someone is wrong — and it is not the buyer.
B2B marketing is stuck in an echo chamber where
everyone uses the same abstract language like
"grow faster, work smarter" without saying
anything specific or differentiating.

---

### Strategy 1 — Crystal Clear Positioning

Move as far away from abstract language as
possible. Abstract example: "Supercharging
your customer delight." Clear example from
Freshworks: "Uncomplicate your IT and customer
service" — this gives a pain point, a use case,
capabilities and social proof all in one line.

The AI test for positioning: copy and paste
your website header into ChatGPT and ask what
you do. If the most powerful machine learning
ever created cannot work out what you do, a
human is not going to figure it out in two
seconds either.

Make positioning:
- Specific and tangible not abstract
- Falsifiable — use real numbers and percentages
- Customer-focused not peer-focused

---

### Strategy 2 — Marketing Before the Sales Cycle

Research from Dream Data analysed 23,000 sessions
and 220,000 B2B company journeys. Key finding:
the average B2B journey from first touch to last
trackable touch is 211 days. And 70% of the
buying decision is shaped before a prospect ever
speaks to a sales rep.

This means: if you only market to people when
they are ready to buy (Google Ads), you are
missing the majority of the decision-making
process.

LinkedIn had the highest return on ad spend
across LinkedIn, Google and Meta for B2B
advertising in this research.

The goal of LinkedIn marketing is creating
SALIENCE — being the brand that comes to mind
when a prospect faces a specific problem. Not
brand awareness. Salience. You want people to
think of you when the pain appears, not just
when they search "best B2B agency."

---

### Strategy 3 — The Fresh Start Effect

A temporal landmark disrupts day-to-day thinking
and opens people to big-picture decisions. In
B2B this shows up as:
- Someone starting a new job
- A company growing at a new rate
- Moving offices
- Budget cycle resets

These are the moments when buyers are most open
to bringing on new vendors. Research shows most
prospects contact companies 3-6 months after
starting a new role — they want to make an
impact while the energy is still fresh.

Target and time outbound around these signals.

---

### Strategy 4 — Vibe Coding for Marketing Tools

The barrier to creating software has never been
lower. If you understand your audience's pain
points deeply, you can use AI to build simple
tools that solve specific problems.

Ben's example: identified gaps in the LinkedIn
Ads platform, built a Chrome extension over a
weekend, shared it on LinkedIn, validated demand,
got a developer to build it properly, and later
won an RFP from a public sector client worth
hundreds of thousands per month in paid media —
all because of that tool.

For 100Hires: building a simple AI hiring tool
or checklist that solves a specific HR pain
point could generate LinkedIn engagement and
establish subject matter authority.

---

### Strategy 5 — Direct Mail as a Super Touch Point

In a world of AI spam and digital noise, physical
direct mail is one of the only "super touch
points" — it creates lasting brand impact AND
drives sales simultaneously. 90% of direct mail
campaigns drive a sale within 6 months (B2C
data, but illustrates attention power).

For B2B, combine direct mail with LinkedIn
engagement signals — send physical mail to
companies that are showing high engagement
with your LinkedIn content.

---

### Strategy 6 — AI Search Optimisation (GEO/LEO)

Appearing in AI answers (ChatGPT, Perplexity,
Gemini) is now part of SEO. Unlike Google which
rewards how you describe yourself, AI rewards
how others describe you — third party trust,
sentiment and citations.

Three areas to focus on:
- Off-site: entity stacking — get listed on
  Wikipedia, Crunchbase, G2, TrustPilot, Reddit,
  directories, PR syndications
- On-site: structure content for AI readability
  — FAQs, clear formatting, allow AI bots to
  crawl the site
- Behavioral: brand building — the more people
  search your brand name, the more AI recommends
  you

---

### Strategy 7 — B2B Influencer Marketing

LinkedIn research: 94% of 3,000 B2B marketers
surveyed globally say influencer marketing was
one of their most effective direct sales channels.
63% said their marketing felt incomplete without
it.

LinkedIn Thought Leadership Ads — use your ad
budget to promote posts from people who talk
about your product. This gives third-party
credibility through paid amplification. Very
powerful for B2B SaaS companies that rely on
peer trust.

---

### Strategy 8 — Inbound Signals to Prompt Outbound

Use LinkedIn Ads analytics to identify companies
that have been highly engaged with your content
in the last 7, 30, 60 or 90 days. LinkedIn rates
engagement as very high, high, medium or low.

Companies showing medium-to-very-high engagement
are your warmest prospects. Use this data to
inform outbound sequences — email, LinkedIn
outreach, or direct mail — timed around their
engagement signals.

---

### Strategy 9 — Google Ads + Competitor Targeting

In saturated markets, the only way to win new
customers is by taking them from competitors.
Rather than bidding on competitor brand names,
target people who are actively using a competitor
product by placing ads on their login pages or
common usage URLs.

Build a remarketing audience of competitor users
over time through LinkedIn. Then market to them
during their fresh start moments when switching
costs feel lowest.

---

### Strategy 10 — Bing Ads

Bing Ads are consistently underused by B2B
companies. Key facts from Ben's agency experience:
- Bing represents about 18% of desktop search
  volume versus Google
- Bing Ads have a higher conversion rate than
  Google Ads in most B2B categories
- Cheaper traffic with better demographics
- Easier to hit impression share targets

Recommended approach: keep Google Ads for
high-intent and competitor alternative campaigns.
Move everything else to Bing and LinkedIn.

---

### Strategy 11 — YouTube Marketing

B2B companies are massively underestimating
YouTube. Long-form video content builds trust
in a way that static ads cannot — when people
watch enough of your content, they remember you
without needing repeated ad exposure.

YouTube content can be repurposed: publish up
to 15 minutes of it directly on LinkedIn and
Meta to reach your exact target audience with
content-native ads that do not even feel like
ads.

## My Observations

Ben B2B's video is the most comprehensive B2B
marketing strategy breakdown I found. Three
insights are directly relevant to 100Hires:

First, the 211-day B2B buying journey stat is
critical. HR managers and startup founders do
not discover an ATS and buy immediately. 100Hires
needs to be building LinkedIn presence for the
long game — being the name that comes to mind
when a founder hits 10 employees and realises
they need a hiring system.

Second, the salience concept directly supports
the LinkedIn organic content strategy. 100Hires
should not be posting to get immediate demos.
They should be posting to own a mental category
— when a founder thinks "hiring," they should
think 100Hires.

Third, the inbound signals strategy is
immediately actionable for 100Hires. Anyone
engaging with their LinkedIn content is a warm
lead. Reaching out to them with context — "I
noticed you engaged with our post about AI
hiring" — will convert at a far higher rate
than cold outreach.
