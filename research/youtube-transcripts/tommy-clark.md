## Core Insights Extracted

### 1. Why LinkedIn for B2B SaaS
LinkedIn has a primarily B2B audience that is
already in the mental frame to consume and act
on business content. Unlike TikTok or Instagram
where users are in entertainment mode, LinkedIn
users are open to being sold to. Only about 1%
of users post actively, creating a massive
opportunity for consistent organic growth.

### 2. Founder-Led Content First
Post from the founder's personal account, not
the company page. Data from the video:
- Shopify's president Harley got double the
  engagement of the official Shopify brand page
- Lavender's co-founder got 130+ likes vs 19
  likes on the company account post
- SparkToro founder Rand Fishkin's video got
  1000+ likes vs 20 likes on the brand post

Personal accounts consistently outperform
company pages on LinkedIn.

### 3. The Content Ecosystem
At scale, build three content layers:
- Founder-led content (highest ROI, start here)
- Company-led content
- Employee-led content

Beehive is cited as the best example — founder
Tyler, company page, and active employee accounts
all working together.

### 4. Account Positioning — One Key Question
Before posting anything, answer:
"Why should someone in my ICP follow this account?"

If you cannot answer this clearly, do not start
posting. Most founders fail because they spam
product updates without giving the ICP a reason
to follow.

### 5. Value-Based vs Entertainment-Based Content
For B2B SaaS founders, lean into value-based
content. content that maps directly to the
problem your product solves. Entertainment-based
content builds bigger audiences but attracts the
wrong people.

The three-part Venn diagram for content success:
- Relevance to your product
- Value to your ICP
- Founder credibility

All three must overlap for content to perform
and convert.

### 6. The Content Calendar — 5 Post Types
Tommy's weekly content calendar (post 5-7x/week):

1. Business-related personal story, personal
   anecdote with a business takeaway tied to
   your product or ICP

2. Story-based listicle, start with a hook
   story, then organise takeaways as a numbered
   list. Creates curiosity and is easy to write.

3. Customer anecdote, "I was on a call" format.
   Take a real conversation and turn it into a
   post with a business insight.

4. Polarizing industry opinion, takes a strong
   stance on something your ICP cares about.
   Drives comments and engagement. Only share
   opinions you genuinely believe in.

5. Case study post — social proof. Keep it
   social-native (no offsite links). Format:
   hook → context → problem → solution → impact
   → CTA. Incorporate once per week.

### 7. Anatomy of a Winning Post
Every post has three parts:
- Hook, spend most time here. If the hook
  fails, nobody reads the rest.
- Body, don't overthink. Let it flow naturally.
  Use listicle format for easy organisation.
- CTA, be direct, not desperate. Tell people
  clearly what to do without sounding salesy.

### 8. Posting Cadence
Post 5-7 times per week, approximately once
per day on weekdays. Posting more than twice
per day can suppress reach on LinkedIn. Posting
once or twice per month is not enough to build
an audience.

### 9. Early Engagement Matters
Get your team to engage with posts in the first
0-30 minutes after publishing. Early engagement
signals to the LinkedIn algorithm that the
content is worth distributing more widely.

### 10. Measuring Success — Two Buckets
Social metrics: impressions, follower growth,
likes, comments, reposts.

Business metrics: inbound leads, email list
growth, self-reported attribution on sales calls
("how did you hear about us?").

Expect 1-3 months before business metrics
(leads) start flowing from LinkedIn content.

## My Observations

Tommy's video is the most directly applicable
resource I found for 100Hires. Three specific
insights stand out:

First, the founder-led content argument is
backed by real data, personal accounts
consistently get 3-5x more engagement than
company pages. For 100Hires, this means the
CEO or founder posting about hiring, HR tech,
and recruitment will outperform the company
page alone.

Second, the "why should my ICP follow this
account" question is critical. 100Hires' ICP
is startup founders and HR managers. The
content must provide value around hiring,
team building, and recruitment, not just
product features.

Third, the 1-3 month lag before business
results appear is important context. LinkedIn
content is a long game. 100Hires should not
expect immediate lead flow, the strategy
needs consistent execution over months before
measuring ROI.
